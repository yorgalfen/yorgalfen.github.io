Help Page

This website is a visualization of the Liebnitz Beta Plateau, near the lunar South Pole, for the 2024 NASA App Development Challenge. View the source files at https://github.com/yorgalfen/yorgalfen.github.io. View info about the challenge at https://www.nasa.gov/learning-resources/app-development-challenge.

Controls:

Click and drag to look around.

WMove forward
AMove left
SMove backward
DMove right
FEnter full screen mode.
LToggle automatic height adjustment. Starts off.
QMove up, if automatic height adjustment is disabled.
EMove down, if automatic height adjustment is disabled.
VChange camera field of view, in degrees. Default is 80.
PInput a position for teleportation. See below.
XChange rendering size. This number is the width, in data points, of the square rendered at one time. Default is 200.
MChange the slope, in degrees, below which squares will be part of the gradient. Default is 15.
NChange the slope, in degrees, above which squares will be part of the gradient. Default is 5.
HDisplay this help page.
EscExit full screen mode.
CChange terrain colorization scheme.
RToggle route planning interface.
UToggle availability of quantum communication.
GChange ambient light intensity, from 0 to 100.

Color Meanings:

The maps shown in both the minimap and the route planning interface are slope maps of the data, with red representing higher slopes and green representing lower slopes.

Blue - current route

Cyan - communication checkpoints along route

If colorization is enabled, then:

Red - Slopes near the high end of the gradient or above the gradient's maximum value.

Green - Slopes near the low end of the gradient or below the gradient's minimum value.

Yellow - Slopes near the middle of the gradient.

Gray - All squares not part of the route.

Red - Squares near the region's minimum elevation of 2796.34 meters.

Green - Squares near the region's maximum elevation of 6463.86 meters.

Yellow - Elevations near the middle of the gradient.

Red - Squares near the region's maximum elevation angle to Earth of 10.529°

Green - Squares near the region's minimum elevation angle to Earth of 9.998°

Yellow - Elevation angles to Earth near the middle of the gradient.

Red - Squares near the region's maximum azimuth angle to Earth of 36.764°

Green - Squares near the region's minimum azimuth angle to Earth of 27.620°

Yellow - Azimuth angles to Earth near the middle of the gradient.

Teleportation:

Input a data file row (subList) or column (index) to go to, input latitude and longitude, or click anywhere on the minimap while the teleportation interface is visible.

-85.7882225 < latitude < -85.0634174

27.289349 < longitude < 36.3651141

Routing:

When the route-displaying canvas is blank, as in the image to left, click on the canvas to select your destination site. Your current location will serve as the starting point. Type a question mark (?) into the max slope field to plan two routes, as explained below. Choose an optimization from the dropdown near the top of the interface.

Optimizations:


Standard - Determine cost by multiplying the distance by a factor linearly scaled from 2.5° to your given max slope.
Distance - Shortest distance between the points, not exceeding the given max slope.
Hill Climbing - Determine cost from the increase in height from one point to the next, and by distance if there is no height increase.
Earth Visibility - Shortest distance between the points, but traveling to a point from which the Earth is not visible is treated as having 10 times the true distance.
The white square is the starting point, and the black square is the ending point. The blue route is that with a maximum slope of 15°, and the brown route has a maximum slope of 20°. The cyan points represent the 10 communication link checkpoints of each route. The statistics are given with the value for the blue route first, then the brown.
